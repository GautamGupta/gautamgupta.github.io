Hello all! I can, (unofficially*)<\/del> announce that I’ve achieved the 5th<\/sup> rank out of the participating 360 students (view rankings<\/a>) in the Google Code-in competition by acquiring 112 points from 34 tasks!<\/p>\n

I’ve tried to put all my thoughts into this single post, so you might find it a bit long, filled with a lot of emoticons. \ud83d\ude1b<\/p>\n

What is Google Code-in? And what does it have to do with me?<\/strong><\/p>\n

From Google Code-in’s FAQ<\/a>:<\/p>\n

Following on from the success of the\u00a0Google Summer of Code<\/a>\u00e2\u201e\u00a2 program, Google is holding a contest for pre-university students (e.g., high school and secondary school students) with the aim of encouraging young people to participate in open source. We will work with open source organizations, each of whom will provide a list of tasks to be completed by student contestants. Tasks can be anything a project needs help with, from bug fixes to writing documentation to user experience research.<\/p><\/blockquote>\n

Coming back to what it has to do with me, I’m a pre-university student enrolled in a high school and 15 years old. Did I just say 15, yes I did! \ud83d\ude42<\/p>\n

I mainly participated in this competition because (and got to know about it when) the organisation WordPress announced that it was participating<\/a> in this competition – and as I’m very much familiar with their code base and especially bbPress<\/a>‘s (because it has been almost more than a year since I’ve been developing on it), I looked forward to this, participated in the competition and finally acquired a good rank. \ud83d\ude09<\/p>\n

And what all did I do in it?<\/strong><\/p>\n

Coding tasks for the bbPress project (and having a lot of fun doing that and bugging John <\/a>James <\/a>Jacoby<\/a>, my mentor, all the time :P) and some translation tasks. As far as I remember, I was the only one (except 1 or 2 – who did a task each) who took up bbPress tasks. So, I feel a bit proud of myself that other people found it difficult \ud83d\ude09 – I know, my assumption might be wrong :P). You may search bbPress Changesets for “props to GautamGupta via GCI” on the bbPress Trac<\/a> itself (while logged in) or go here<\/a> to see the list of tasks done by me. \ud83d\ude09<\/p>\n

And what all did I achieve?<\/strong><\/p>\n

    \n
  1. Knowledge – Really, I didn’t know all the things I did at first and didn’t have that pace which I acquired later – I tried, I did and I learnt new things.<\/li>\n
  2. Experience – The experience working under a<\/del> the cool mentor John<\/a> James<\/a> Jacoby<\/a>. \ud83d\ude09<\/li>\n
  3. Trip to Google Headquarters, Mountain View, California as I came in the top 10! \ud83d\ude00<\/li>\n
  4. 500 USD and a Google T-shirt (more details about the prizes here<\/a>).<\/li>\n
  5. And as a I mentioned earlier, I got the 5th<\/sup> rank out of the participating 360 students (view rankings<\/a>) by acquiring 112 points from 34 tasks though I had expected myself to come in the top 4 (first three were way ahead), I couldn’t because of my school work etc.<\/li>\n<\/ol>\n

      \n
    1. The first thing on my to-do is writing some tutorial blog posts, of the things I did in the tasks<\/del>. Done: #1<\/a>, more to come.<\/li>\n
    2. Complete the last task which I wasn’t able to due to time restrictions – forum reply counts.<\/del> Done<\/a>.<\/li>\n
    3. Contribute more.<\/del> \ud83d\ude09 Doing. (Forever)<\/li>\n
