How to pitch your product to the investors \u2014 this was the question answered by the leading personalities in the field \u2014 Alok Mittal from Canaan Partners and\u00a0Mayank Khanduja from SAIF Partners in today’s session in Delhi.<\/p>\n

Before preparing a pitch, you need to ask yourself \u2014 what’s the traction that your product has got \u2014 how accepted is it? Even if you’ve 500 initial customers, what’s the response of the customers? Are they coming back?\u00a0How are you getting customers \u2014 staff picks on app markets? PR? Offline model? You should also know about the investor. What is his or her background. What kind of startups they’ve invested in, and then appropriately pitch to the investors who you think would be interested in your product.<\/p>\n

You need to build a\u00a0spirit of the pitch<\/strong>. Your pitch is to\u00a0sell<\/em>\u00a0– sell a\u00a0story<\/em>\u00a0– not people or data. Give out some customer testimonials that you’ve got. Have some\u00a0hooks on the pitch<\/strong>\u00a0\u2014 the first thing you’re selling is the opportunity \u2014 the problem that you’re solving. You need to show that you have the appropriate team \u2014 and you would be able to execute it \u2014 with evidence. Do remember that you shouldn’t have any artificial figures in your pitch. This is how you should go about a pitch:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Introduction \u2014 a brief about your product \u2014 what problem does it solve.<\/li>\n
  • Competition \u2014 You need to communicate that you’ll win in the existing market. Showcase how \u2014 what is that that you offer is special.<\/li>\n
  • Team \u2014 Show that you’ve the best position to win. Why are you, and your foundation team there in the startup? Never say that you currently have some advisors whose name you can not reveal and would join in once you get the funding \u2014 the investors do not take away anything positive from that with them.<\/li>\n
  • Monetization \u2014 Show some pricing plans that you’ve thought of. If there are no revenue streams right now, will you have a subscription based pricing model or on the same lines in the future? Do remember that either you’ve got to have a lot (tens of millions) of people and then decide the monetization strategy or say that you’ve some people who would pay \u2014 not that you’ve some people and still need to figure out how they would pay.<\/li>\n
  • Financials \u2014 Tell about your revenue projections, why you need the funding and how you’ll utilise the funds.<\/li>\n
  • Finally, always end on a high \u2014 tell some customer testimonials or some awards that you’ve won for your product.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Elevator Pitch<\/strong><\/p>\n

    You also need an elevator pitch \u2014 a two minute pitch in which you essentially tell the investor why he should give you 20 minutes more to hear about your product.<\/p>\n

      \n
    • Tell about the problem that you’re solving \u2014 the\u00a0what<\/strong>. Have the hooks in it.<\/li>\n
    • How<\/strong>\u00a0are you planning to solve it?<\/li>\n
    • Who<\/strong>\u00a0is your customer?<\/li>\n
    • Do\u00a0not<\/em>\u00a0have any tech\u00a0jargons in the elevator pitch.<\/em><\/li>\n
    • Keep it straight and simple.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
